[h3]- About Serigne Mbacke Bousso:
[/h3]
He is[i] Muhammed Bin Muhammed El Mbousoby[/i], known as [b][i]Cheikh Mbacke Bousso[/i][/b]. His mother is [i]Lady Fatima Mbacke Bint Habibul-lah,[/i] known as [i]Mame Faty Balla[/i], the sister of [i]Mame Mor Anta Saly[/i], who is the father of [b][i]Sheikh Ahmadou Bamba[/i][/b]. 
He was born in 1874 in a village called [i]Mboussobe[/i] in Jolof, raised and educated in a righteous Islamic environment.


[h3]- His Journey:
[/h3]
He memorized the Quran at a young age and studied the basic Islamic sciences under his father, [i]Muhammadou Bousso[/i], known as “[i]Serigne Mbousobe.[/i]” Then he continued his studies under “[i]Sheikh Ahmadou Bamba[/i],” from whom he learned various Islamic sciences like [b]monotheism[/b] (Tawhid), [b]jurisprudence [/b](Fiq), and [b]Sufism[/b]. Then he moved to “[i]Serigne Samba Touklor Ka[/i]” with the permission of the Sheikh to continue his study.

He is considered to be one of the leading scholars in [b]Senegal[/b] in his time, as he mastered more than twenty different sciences, such as: jurisprudence, doctrine, Arabic grammar, Sufism, astronomy, math, and more, and he has written many works in these fields, as he mostly concentrated on what is needed in his country and region as he has several writings in different fields.

[i]Sheikh Ahmadou Bamba[/i] appointed him as the [u]Mufti  [/u]and the judge in Islamic matters, given the trust he had in him and his knowledge, and he performed that well, as he was known for his daring to speak the truth.
He was also given the mission of the correction of the Qibla of the “[b]Great Mosque of Touba[/b],” and he did a great job, turning its qibla directly to Mecca as one of the most accurate Qiblas until toaday.


[h3]- His Description:
[/h3]
[i]Serigne Modou Bousso[/i]  (his elder son) says, [quote]“He was of medium height, slender in build, dark in complexion, with a pleasant and resonant voice. He was eloquent in speech and articulate in expression. When he delivered sermons, eyes would shed tears. He was deliberate and cautious in his affairs, a mountain of knowledge unshaken by storms. He was often silent, constantly reflective and contemplative, and devoted to fasting and night prayers.”[/quote]
He also says about his qualities: [quote]“He preferred obscurity, was extremely modest, constantly withdrawn, and ever deep in thought. His time was always well spent; he was never found wasting time. He was strict with himself, his children, and his close relatives, yet gentle and kind with strangers and outsiders.”[/quote]


[h3]- Guédé:
[/h3]
His village, [b]Guédé Buso[/b] in [b]Touba[/b], is one of the most famous knowledge and Islamic centers in Senegal, known as “[i]The School of Guede[/i],” and till now, many people have come to this place to study Islamic sciences and more, especially Astronomy. This village was founded by [i]Sheikh Mbacke Bousso[/i] in [b]1918 [/b]with the permission of [i]Sheikh Al Khadim[/i].


[h3]- His Last Days:
[/h3]
After coming back from a long trip to different regions in the world in 1928 and after a pilgrimage to [b]Mecca[/b], he came back with a high fever that caused him to stay in bed for the rest of his years. His son, [i]Serigne Modou Bousso[/i], says: [quote]"The heat of the land of Hijaz had seeped into his body, flaring up at times and subsiding at others. We treated him by the will of Allah with tried and tested medicines, taking the means while relying upon the Lord. He remained for years in a state of illness and weakness, his body frail and growing ever thinner, his bones increasingly feeble, until he passed into the mercy of Allah Almighty in the middle of the year 1365 hijry, corresponding to the year 1946, at the age of eighty-four in Guédé. " [/quote]
